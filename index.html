Product Pitch Deck

The slides below walk through the vision, strategy, and roadmap for the Greater Toledo Art Map — from the problem we're solving to the features we're building, the people we're building for, and the metrics that will tell us we got it right.

Slide 1 — The Problem

The Problem

Public art is one of the most accessible forms of creative expression — yet it remains largely invisible to the communities it serves.

Discoverability

No unified, searchable database of public art exists. Travelers and longtime residents alike rarely know what's around the corner.

Missing Context

Without knowing an artist's name, intent, or the cultural moment behind a piece, viewers experience art as decoration — not dialogue.

Preservation

Public artworks are ephemeral. Murals get painted over, sculptures demolished — with no digital record they ever existed.

The Opportunity

What if we used public art to drive people into our restaurants and events? Art already draws foot traffic — connecting it to nearby businesses and local happenings could turn murals into economic engines for the neighborhoods they live in.

Current solutions — scattered Instagram hashtags, city portals, niche walking apps — are fragmented and lack community participation.

Slide 5 — Tech Stack

Technical Architecture

Cloud-native architecture emphasizing scalability, offline resilience, and developer velocity.

Frontend

Cross-platform mobile with React Native (iOS & Android) and Next.js for web. Shared component library via a custom design system.

React Native Next.js Mapbox GL JS Supercluster

Backend

Node.js API Gateway routing to microservices: Artwork, User, Search, and Media services. REST + GraphQL hybrid API.

Node.js GraphQL REST API Microservices

Infrastructure

AWS-powered: containers via ECS, PostgreSQL with PostGIS for spatial queries, S3 + CloudFront for media delivery, ElasticSearch for search, and Redis for caching.

Slide 6 — Design

Design Philosophy

Three core principles guide every pixel of ArtMap's experience.

Art First

The UI recedes to let art shine. Minimal chrome, dark-mode support, full-bleed photos, and generous whitespace ensure artwork is always the hero.

Context as Delight

Information reveals progressively — a thumbnail, then a title, then a rich story. Users are rewarded for curiosity, never overwhelmed.

Community Connection

Art brings people together. Every piece is a conversation starter, connecting visitors with artists and the stories behind the work.

Slide 7 — Roadmap

Product Roadmap

An 18-month plan from foundation to global intelligence.

Phase 1 — Foundation

Months 1–6
  • Launch web MVP focused on Toledo and surrounding communities
  • Seed database with 100+ artworks via Toledo Arts Commission partnerships
  • Core map, search, artwork detail, and user contribution flows
  • Verified artist profile program with founding cohort of 20 local artists

Phase 2 — Community & Expansion

Months 7–12
  • Expand coverage to Perrysburg, Maumee, Sylvania, and greater Lucas County
  • Launch self-guided tour builder for user-created walking routes
  • Introduce social layer: follows, saves, comments, and sharing
  • "Lost Art" archive — document and preserve demolished artworks

Phase 3 — Depth & Intelligence

Months 13–18
  • AR overlay: point camera at public art to surface info via on-device ML
  • AI-generated context: auto-suggest artist bios and historical notes
  • Complete coverage of Wood, Fulton, and Ottawa counties
  • Open API for third-party developers, academics, and city planners
