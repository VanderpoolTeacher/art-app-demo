The Problem

Public art is one of the most accessible forms of creative expression — yet it remains largely invisible to the communities it serves.

Discoverability

No unified, searchable database of public art exists. Travelers and longtime residents alike rarely know what's around the corner.

Missing Context

Without knowing an artist's name, intent, or the cultural moment behind a piece, viewers experience art as decoration — not dialogue.

Preservation

Public artworks are ephemeral. Murals get painted over, sculptures demolished — with no digital record they ever existed.

Current solutions — scattered Instagram hashtags, city portals, niche walking apps — are fragmented and lack community participation.

Design Philosophy

Three core principles guide every pixel of ArtMap's experience.

🖼️

Art First

The UI recedes to let art shine. Minimal chrome, dark-mode support, full-bleed photos, and generous whitespace ensure artwork is always the hero.

✨

Context as Delight

Information reveals progressively — a thumbnail, then a title, then a rich story. Users are rewarded for curiosity, never overwhelmed.

🤝

Community Ownership

Contribution flows feel as natural as social media. Anyone can add; trust is built through reputation, not gatekeeping.

🛡️

Trust & Safety

A robust moderation pipeline ensures data quality and community health.

Contribution Workflow

1

Submit

User submits artwork with at least one photo, GPS coordinates, and a title.

2

Screen

AI-assisted pre-screening flags duplicate coordinates, blurry images, or policy violations.

3

Review

Community vetters (Curators) review flagged or new submissions using earned trust.

4

Approve

Submissions meeting quality criteria auto-approve within 24 hours; edge cases go to admin.

Reputation System

⭐

Earn Points

Contributors earn points for approved submissions, verified edits, and helpful comments.

🏅

Unlock Curator

Reaching 500 points unlocks Curator status with expanded review permissions.

✅

Verified Artists

Artists receive a badge and can manage their own works without moderation delay.

Product Roadmap

An 18-month plan from foundation to global intelligence.

Phase 1 — Foundation

Months 1–6
  • Launch iOS & Android MVP in NYC, LA, and Chicago
  • Seed database with 5,000 artworks via city arts partnerships
  • Core map, search, artwork detail, and user contribution flows
  • Verified artist profile program with founding cohort of 100 artists

Phase 2 — Community & Expansion

Months 7–12
  • Expand to 10 additional US cities + London, Mexico City, Sao Paulo
  • Launch self-guided tour builder for user-created walking routes
  • Introduce social layer: follows, saves, comments, and sharing
  • "Lost Art" archive — document and preserve demolished artworks

Phase 3 — Depth & Intelligence

Months 13–18
  • AR overlay: point camera at public art to surface info via on-device ML
  • AI-generated context: auto-suggest artist bios and historical notes
  • Global expansion to 50+ cities with multilingual support
  • Open API for third-party developers, academics, and city planners

Risks & Mitigations

Click each risk to reveal the mitigation strategy.

Low-quality user submissions degrade trust

High ▼

AI pre-screening combined with a tiered contributor reputation system and community flagging tools ensures quality at scale.

IP disputes over photographed artworks

Medium ▼

Clear photography rights policy displayed prominently. Artist partnerships for explicit consent. Adherence to U.S. VARA guidelines.

Map data licensing cost overruns

Medium ▼

Negotiate startup tier with Mapbox. Evaluate OpenStreetMap as a fallback for non-critical map views.

Slow initial data coverage limiting user value

High ▼

Pre-seed database via data partnerships before launch. Focus depth over breadth in initial launch cities to ensure a rich experience from day one.

Competition from Google Maps or large incumbents

Low ▼

Focus on the depth of art-specific context and community that general-purpose platforms cannot replicate. Build a moat through cultural trust.
